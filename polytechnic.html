What is a Polytechnic Course?

A polytechnic course is a progressive learning experience that allows students to participate in a diploma or vocational course based on technical education. These courses provide a viable alternative to degree programmes, and students may pursue them after completing their secondary education. Polytechnic courses focus on developing skills and practical experience rather than theoretical knowledge. Upon completion, students receive a diploma that typically qualifies them to apply for jobs in their desired field.



Polytechnic Course Subject Areas

Government-owned and privately owned polytechnic schools provide a variety of course options. In general, the courses may provide students with a diploma in a technical area, such as engineering, or a diploma in a non-technical domain, such as the arts and sciences. Many schools continue to provide more polytechnic course options to pursue.
Some specific examples of potential polytechnic course subject areas include:

  • Aeronautical engineering
  • Aerospace engineering
  • Agricultural engineering
  • Agricultural information technology
  • Art and craft
  • Automobile engineering
  • Biotechnology engineering
  • Chemical engineering
  • Civil engineering
  • Computer science and engineering
  • Dairy technology and engineering
  • Electrical engineering
  • Electronics and communication
  • Electronics engineering
  • Electronics and telecommunication engineering
  • Environmental engineering
  • Infrastructure engineering
  • Instrumentation and control engineering
  • Mechanical engineering
