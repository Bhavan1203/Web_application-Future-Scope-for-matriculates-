Paramedical Course


Paramedical courses are profession-oriented medical training courses that enable students to develop a career in the medical industry in a short amount of time and at a low cost. Candidates can enter the medical industry through Paramedical courses in areas such as medical emergency, aid, and diagnostics. Paramedical courses refer to a specialized field of study that focuses on providing healthcare support services. The Paramedical course is designed to train individuals in various medical procedures and techniques, primarily to assist doctors and other healthcare professionals in delivering optimal patient care. The average course fees of Paramedical courses range from INR 35,000 to INR 60,000. Candidates, who have passed Class 12 in Science with Biology, are eligible to join Paramedical courses. The basic Paramedical course qualifying criteria are 50% in class 12 (45% in the case of SC/ST).
Paramedical courses cover a wide range of disciplines, including but not limited to Nursing, Physiotherapy, Medical Laboratory Technology, Radiography, Occupational Therapy, and Optometry. Paramedical courses are typically offered at vocational training institutes, colleges, and universities.

Paramedical science is a branch of science that provides services and professions that complement and support medical work but do not require the full qualifications of a physician, such as nursing, radiography, first aid, physical therapy, and dietetics. This article aims to introduce students to the various courses in this field. This discipline of medicine plays a very important role in the treatment of any type of patient or disease, also they are professionally oriented academic programs, so making a profession in this field is not a bad idea at all. Students of the 12th scientific and biological group can enroll in these courses.
